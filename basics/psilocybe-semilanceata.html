Psilocybe semilanceata

Essential natural history and field notes for the Liberty Cap.

Morphological Snapshot

  • Cap: 0.5–2.5 cm wide, sharply conical with a pronounced umbo, becoming more bell-shaped with age.
  • Gills: Adnexed to nearly free, initially pale grey turning purple-brown as spores mature.
  • Stipe: 4–10 cm long, slender, flexible, sometimes slightly translucent when moist.

Habitat & Distribution

  • Region: Temperate zones across Europe, North America, and parts of South America and New Zealand.
  • Substrate: Nutrient-rich grasslands, especially sheep or cattle pastures with unimproved soils.
  • Fruiting Season: Autumn through early winter, following sustained rains and cool nights.

Historical Context

Documented in the scientific literature since 1838, Psilocybe semilanceata has long been associated with pastoral landscapes. Its psychoactive properties were popularized in the 20th century, influencing modern ethnomycology.

Forager Notes

  • Fruits often appear in troops or arcs across grassy slopes.
  • Caps remain viscid in wet weather; carrying waxed paper prevents sticking.
  • Always double-check for deadly Galerina when specimens occur near woody debris.
