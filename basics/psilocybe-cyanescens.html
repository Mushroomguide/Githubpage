Psilocybe cyanescens

Overview of the Wavy Cap's ecology, morphology, and range.

Morphological Snapshot

  • Cap: 1.5–5 cm wide, chestnut-brown when moist, fading to buff with rippled margins.
  • Gills: Adnate to adnexed, close, transitioning from pale to dark purple-brown with age.
  • Stipe: 3–9 cm long, fibrous, often curved with dense white mycelium at the base.

Habitat & Distribution

  • Region: Coastal temperate zones of the Pacific Northwest, Western Europe, and scattered urban plantings worldwide.
  • Substrate: Hardwood and conifer mulch, garden beds, landscaping bark, occasionally sawdust piles.
  • Fruiting Season: Late autumn through early winter; sometimes spring flushes in cool climates.

Historical Context

Described in 1946, Psilocybe cyanescens has proliferated alongside the landscaping industry. Its affinity for wood chips makes it a common urban discovery, prompting numerous field studies on invasive distributions.

Forager Notes

  • Fruits in dense clusters, often under rhododendron, ivy, or ornamental shrubs.
  • Mulch beds that were recently refreshed may fruit heavily after the first frost.
  • Monitor local regulations; some municipalities treat intentional cultivation as illegal.
