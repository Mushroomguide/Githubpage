Panaeolus cinctulus

Key traits and ecology of the banded mottlegill.

Morphological Snapshot

  • Cap: 1.5–5 cm, hemispherical to convex, drying to tan with a darker encircling band.
  • Gills: Adnate, mottled appearance caused by asynchronous spore maturation.
  • Stipe: 4–12 cm, slender, smooth, sometimes showing faint blue tones near the base.

Habitat & Distribution

  • Region: Cosmopolitan; common across North America, Europe, Australia, and temperate Asia.
  • Substrate: Compost heaps, well-fertilized lawns, horse stables, and rich garden beds.
  • Fruiting Season: Spring through autumn, often after irrigation or rainfall following warm days.

Historical Context

Formerly known as Panaeolus subbalteatus, this species was once considered a greenhouse weed. It has adapted well to human-managed soils and is now one of the most frequently encountered Panaeolus worldwide.

Forager Notes

  • Look for rings of mushrooms on compost-rich lawns, especially near sprinkler heads.
  • Caps may crack in dry weather; rehydrate specimens in situ to confirm color changes.
  • Exercise caution around playgrounds and public parks where chemical treatments may occur.
