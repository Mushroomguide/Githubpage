Psilocybe semilanceata

Liberty Caps are tiny yet distinctive. Use these comparisons to separate them from dangerous or unwanted species.

Key Identifiers

  • Cap: Conical to bell-shaped with a sharp papilla, translucent-striate when moist, caramel to olive-brown.
  • Stipe: Slender, flexible, typically without an annulus, bruises blue-green slowly.
  • Spore Print: Dark purple-brown.

Common Lookalikes

  • Panaeolina foenisecii (Mower's Mushroom) Spore print is tobacco-brown; caps lack the pointed papilla and blue bruising. Flesh remains pale when handled.
  • Conocybe apala Extremely fragile with pure white stalks and caps that collapse as they dry. Gills are cinnamon-brown and the cap surface is smooth, lacking the gelatinous feel of Psilocybe.
  • Galerina marginata Deadly; typically grows on wood, features a ring zone on the stipe, and has rusty-orange spores. Cap margin often translucent-striate but without the elastic, twisting stipe of Liberty Caps.

Field Techniques

  • Habitat Context: Liberty Caps emerge from unimproved pasture grasses, rarely on dung or wood.
  • Cap Texture: Gently roll the cap; a gelatinous pellicle should separate from the flesh when pinched.
  • Spore Print: Collect overnight on foil to confirm the deep violet-brown deposit.
