Panaeolus cinctulus

Band-Ringed Panaeolus often fruits in lawns and compost piles. Distinguish it from the numerous brown lawn mushrooms.

Key Identifiers

  • Cap: Chestnut to tan with a persistent dark band along the margin when moist.
  • Stipe: Pale tan, sometimes slightly thickened at the base, lacking a ring.
  • Spore Print: Jet black.

Common Lookalikes

  • Panaeolina foenisecii Spore print is brown, not black. Gills become mottled but never inky black, and caps are more fragile without a strong marginal band.
  • Panaeolus fimicola Very similar but usually darker overall with a more slender stipe; microscopy of spores (smooth vs. roughened) provides certainty.
  • Psathyrella species Often hygrophanous with brittle stems that snap easily. Spore prints are brown and caps lack the distinct two-toned band.

Field Techniques

  • Spore Print Verification: Lay caps on white paper; the deposit should be sooty black within six hours.
  • Gill Pattern: Look for the mottled gills characteristic of Panaeolus—patches of mature and immature spores create the speckled effect.
  • Growth Medium: P. cinctulus favors rich compost or horse manure mixed into lawns, often after rain and warm evenings.
