Amanita pantherina

The Panther Cap is highly toxic. Careful observation of its universal veil remnants and volva is essential.

Key Identifiers

  • Cap: Chocolate to olive-brown with stark white warts; margin lined with striations.
  • Stipe: White with a skirt-like ring and bulbous base encased in a rolled, collar-like volva.
  • Flesh: White, not staining when cut; odor mild to slightly earthy.

Common Lookalikes

  • Amanita rubescens Edible when cooked but easily confused. Rubescens bruises pinkish-red on cap and gills, and its warts are greyish rather than bright white.
  • Amanita gemmata Smaller, with yellow-ochre caps and a fragile ring. The bulbous base lacks the sharply defined rim seen on Pantherina.
  • Amanita muscaria Has scarlet to orange caps and a layered volva with concentric rings. Pantherina remains brown and its volva forms a single rolled collar.

Field Techniques

  • Excavate the Base: Carefully dig around the stem to expose the volva shape—Pantherina shows a prominent rim.
  • Check Wart Attachment: Warts are easily brushed off, revealing smooth brown cuticle beneath.
  • Record Habitat: Typically forms mycorrhiza with conifers and beech in temperate forests.
