Psilocybe cyanescens

Wavy Caps flourish on wood chips. Compare them carefully against other decomposers before harvesting.

Key Identifiers

  • Cap: Chestnut-brown with wavy margins, hygrophanous, bruising deep indigo where handled.
  • Stipe: Cream to buff, fibrous, often with mycelial fuzz at the base and strong blue bruising.
  • Spore Print: Violet-brown to purplish black.

Common Lookalikes

  • Galerina marginata Fatal species; exhibits a distinct membranous ring, rusty brown spores, and rarely bruises blue. Flesh is brittle compared with the elastic Psilocybe stipe.
  • Hypholoma fasciculare (Sulphur Tuft) Grows in dense clusters with sulphur-yellow gills that turn olive-green, lacks any blue bruising, and often tastes sharply bitter. Spore deposit is dingy brown rather than deep violet.
  • Leratiomyces ceres Brick-red cap with a sticky surface and pale tan stalk. Spore print is dark brown, but gills retain rusty tones and the flesh never stains blue.

Field Techniques

  • Check the Margin: Mature Wavy Caps have undulating edges; young specimens may be round but still bruise strongly.
  • Inspect the Substrate: True Psilocybe cyanescens prefers lignin-rich wood chips, especially alder or conifer mixes.
  • Confirm Bruising: Lightly pinch the stipe base; vibrant blue staining should develop within minutes.
