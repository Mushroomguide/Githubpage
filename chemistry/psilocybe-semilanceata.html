Psilocybe semilanceata

Chemical profile of the Liberty Cap and considerations for potency, metabolism, and toxicity.

Principal Constituents

Compound Typical Range Notes
Psilocybin 0.7 – 1.5% dry weight Primary prodrug converting to psilocin; Liberty Caps rank among the most potent Psilocybe species.
Psilocin 0.05 – 0.3% dry weight Labile compound responsible for blue bruising when oxidized.
Baeocystin Trace – 0.4% dry weight Methyl analog of psilocybin; pharmacology less characterized.

Pharmacology & Metabolism

  • Psilocybin is rapidly dephosphorylated to psilocin in the gut and liver via alkaline phosphatase.
  • Psilocin acts as a partial agonist at 5-HT2A serotonin receptors, altering sensory processing.
  • Onset of effects typically occurs within 20–45 minutes when ingested.

Toxicology

  • Acute Risks: Nausea, anxiety, and sympathetic activation (elevated heart rate, mild hypertension).
  • Interactions: Contraindicated with MAO inhibitors, SSRIs, and tricyclic antidepressants without medical oversight.
  • Overdose Considerations: High doses can lead to confusion, ataxia, and in rare cases prolonged psychosis-like reactions.
