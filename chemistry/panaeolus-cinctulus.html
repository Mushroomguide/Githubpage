Panaeolus cinctulus

A common lawn mushroom with variable psychoactive alkaloid content.

Principal Constituents

Compound Typical Range Notes
Psilocybin 0.1 – 0.4% dry weight Levels fluctuate dramatically; many collections are nearly inactive.
Psilocin Trace – 0.1% dry weight Responsible for occasional blue bruising along the stipe.
Serotonin Derivatives Trace Includes 5-HT and 5-HTP; significance to human effects is uncertain.

Pharmacology & Metabolism

  • Because potency is inconsistent, effects range from negligible to mild psychoactivity.
  • Metabolism mirrors other psilocybin-containing species: hepatic conversion to psilocin via dephosphorylation.
  • Reports of nausea are more common, possibly due to co-occurring lawn fungi or ingested substrate.

Toxicology

  • Acute Risks: Gastrointestinal upset is frequent; rare cases of disorientation reported.
  • Contaminants: Lawns treated with pesticides or herbicides can introduce additional toxins—avoid areas with chemical application.
  • Misidentification: Non-psychoactive Panaeolina and toxic lawn species may be mixed in harvests.
