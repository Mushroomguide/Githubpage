Amanita muscaria

Fly Agaric chemistry centers on ibotenic acid and muscimol, modulated by regional variations.

Principal Constituents

Compound Typical Range Notes
Ibotenic Acid 0.03 – 0.1% fresh weight Precursor to muscimol; levels higher in caps than stipes.
Muscimol 0.01 – 0.05% fresh weight Primary psychoactive agent after decarboxylation.
Muscarine Trace Contributes minimally to symptomology but may cause perspiration and salivation.

Pharmacology & Metabolism

  • Ibotenic acid is partially converted to muscimol during drying at moderate heat.
  • Muscimol is excreted largely unmetabolized via urine within 24 hours.
  • Regional chemotypes (var. guessowii, var. flavivolvata) show differing ratios of ibotenic acid to muscimol.

Toxicology

  • Acute Risks: Ataxia, alternating agitation and lethargy, vomiting, and hallucinosis.
  • Delayed Effects: Sleep followed by confusion or delirium; retrograde amnesia is common.
  • Medical Advice: Seek emergency care after ingestion; supportive monitoring is standard treatment.
