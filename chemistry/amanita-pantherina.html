Amanita pantherina

Panther Caps contain neurotoxic isoxazoles requiring extreme caution.

Principal Constituents

Compound Typical Range Notes
Ibotenic Acid 0.15 – 0.6% fresh weight Neurotoxic agonist at glutamate receptors; decarboxylates to muscimol when heated or dried.
Muscimol 0.02 – 0.2% fresh weight Potent GABAA agonist producing sedative-hypnotic effects.
Muscarine Trace Present in minute amounts; cholinergic symptoms are usually mild compared with Clitocybe/Inocybe poisonings.

Pharmacology & Metabolism

  • Ibotenic acid is partially converted to muscimol during drying or cooking; both cross the blood-brain barrier.
  • Muscimol binds to GABAA receptors leading to dissociation, delirium, and ataxia.
  • Onset occurs 30 minutes to 2 hours post-ingestion with effects lasting 6–8 hours.

Toxicology

  • Acute Risks: Severe confusion, muscle twitching, vomiting, and potential coma in high doses.
  • Medical Response: Supportive care; activated charcoal may reduce absorption. Benzodiazepines sometimes used to manage agitation.
  • Fatality Risk: Deaths are rare but documented, especially in children or when combined with depressants.
